Directions and parking at MBC Tysons

Directions from Google

Driving

  • From the Beltway (I-495) take Exit 47A, Route 7 west towards Tysons Corner. After you cross the Dulles Toll Road (267), look for McLean Bible Church on the left after about 6/10 mile. Turn left at the traffic light (Lewinsville Road) into the MBC parking lot.
  • From the west, take Leesburg Pike (Rt. 7) to Lewinsville Road & turn right at the light.
  • From the Dulles Toll Road, take the Leesburg Pike exit west (Exit 16) & go towards Leesburg. MBC is on the left at the light at Lewinsville Road.

Parking

  • MBC Tysons has a 2-level parking garage. Parking is free
  • Enter the church property at the intersection at Leesburg Pike and Lewinsville Road.
  • For easy access to the Main Auditorium (for main worship services), keep left and drive up the ramp to the second floor.
  • For easy access to the Smith Center (for The Edge and Citywide), keep to the right and enter the garage on the first floor.

Metro

  • Take the Silver Line and get off at Spring Hill Metro Stop
  • Get on Fairfax Connector Bus 574, Reston Town Center - Tysons
  • Arrive at MBC Tysons, 8925 Leesburg Pike, Vienna, VA 22182.
  • Visit fairfaxconnector.com for more information and bus schedule.

Come Join Us for the Pre-conference Trauma Healing Event

Thursday June 22, 2017: Time 2:00pm to 4:00pm

Healing the Wounds of Trauma at Home and Abroad

Desiree Guyton and Ray Hutchison: SIM USA

“He heals the brokenhearted and binds up their wounds.” Ps. 147:3 (ESV)

“Trauma is the new mission field,” by Dr. Diane Langberg, PH.D

Description: Trauma refers to the psychological and emotional effects of human being experiencing disruptive events like sexual abuse, urban violence, abandonment, domestic violence and etc. This interactive and scripture engaging workshop will give a brief overview of trauma and how the church can be a healing community at home and abroad. The workshop introduces participants to a program that uses biblical and mental health techniques that allows traumatized people to experience the life changing message of the bible. Trauma Healing Institute serves the world’s largest people group overseas and in the United States. “1 in 7 people have experienced trauma that prevents them from encountering God in the Bible,” – American Bible Society Website
